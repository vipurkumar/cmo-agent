# Objection Handling Guide

Approved responses for the most common objections encountered in outbound and sales conversations. Use these as templates — adapt tone and specifics to the prospect's context.

---

## Objection: "We built this internally"

**Why they say it**: Engineering-led organizations often build internal tools for outbound automation, list management, or CRM enrichment. They see it as a sunk cost and a competitive advantage.

**Response framework**:

1. **Acknowledge the investment**: "That's impressive — building internal GTM tooling shows real commitment to your revenue engine."
2. **Probe for pain**: "How much engineering time goes into maintaining it? And does it keep up with the pace of new data sources and channels?"
3. **Reframe the cost**: "What we consistently hear from companies that built internally is that maintenance becomes the bottleneck. Every API change, every new enrichment source, every new sequence type requires engineering cycles. OmniGTM handles that infrastructure so your engineers can ship product."
4. **Proof point**: "Acme SaaS had a homegrown outbound system maintained by 2 engineers. After switching to OmniGTM, those engineers moved back to product work, and outbound volume increased 3.4x."

**Key message**: Internal tools create engineering debt. OmniGTM is maintained by a team whose only job is GTM automation.

## Objection: "We just signed with [Competitor X]"

**Why they say it**: They've recently committed budget and political capital to a competing solution. Switching feels costly and embarrassing.

**Response framework**:

1. **Respect the decision**: "Makes sense — you did your diligence and made a call. How's the rollout going so far?"
2. **Plant a seed**: "What we've found is that most GTM tools handle one piece — maybe sequencing or enrichment — but not the full loop. If you hit a wall on personalization quality or multi-channel orchestration, we'd love to be the second call."
3. **Offer a benchmark**: "Would it be useful to see how your current reply rates and pipeline metrics compare to what our customers see? No commitment — just data."
4. **Set a timeline**: "Most contracts in this space are 12 months. Can I check back in Q3 to see how things are tracking?"

**Key message**: Don't fight the decision. Establish credibility, plant doubt about completeness, and stay in touch.

## Objection: "Not a priority right now"

**Why they say it**: They may genuinely have higher priorities, or they don't yet see the cost of inaction on GTM execution.

**Response framework**:

1. **Validate their priorities**: "Totally fair — you know your roadmap better than anyone. What's the top priority right now?"
2. **Connect to their priority**: "Interesting — [their priority] is actually something our customers tackle in parallel. For example, FinTech Co deployed OmniGTM alongside their billing migration and had automated outbound running before billing was fully live."
3. **Quantify the cost of waiting**: "Every quarter without automated outbound is roughly [X] hours of manual work and [Y]% lower reply rates compared to AI-personalized sequences. For a team your size, that's approximately [Z] pipeline dollars left on the table."
4. **Offer a low-commitment next step**: "What if I send over a 2-minute video showing what the first week looks like? No call needed — just so it's on your radar when priorities shift."

**Key message**: Waiting has a measurable cost. Make it easy to re-engage later.

## Objection: "Too expensive"

**Why they say it**: They may be comparing to the cost of doing nothing (not the cost of manual effort), or they have genuine budget constraints.

**Response framework**:

1. **Understand the comparison**: "Expensive compared to what? The manual effort today, or a different tool you're evaluating?"
2. **Reframe as ROI**: "OmniGTM costs [price]. Acme SaaS generated $1.2M in influenced pipeline in 90 days. FinTech Co saw $3.8M in new qualified pipeline in 120 days. The question isn't the cost of the tool — it's the cost of not having it."
3. **Break down unit economics**: "The average LLM cost per session is under $0.08. Each session does the equivalent of 30-45 minutes of BDR research and writing. At a fully-loaded BDR cost of $80K+/year, OmniGTM pays for itself if it replaces even 10% of manual effort."
4. **Offer a pilot**: "What if we started with a single campaign — 50 accounts, 2 weeks — so you can see the ROI before committing to a full rollout?"

**Key message**: OmniGTM is not a cost center. It's a pipeline multiplier with measurable, fast ROI. Always anchor to pipeline generated, not tool cost.

---

## General Principles for Objection Handling

1. **Never dismiss the objection**. Acknowledge it genuinely before responding.
2. **Ask before telling**. A question reveals the real concern behind the stated objection.
3. **Use specific numbers**. Vague claims ("we're better") lose to specific metrics ("52% reduction in cycle time").
4. **Offer a low-friction next step**. Never push for a demo when a data point or case study will do.
5. **Know when to walk away**. If timing is genuinely wrong, set a follow-up and move on. Persistence without value is spam.
