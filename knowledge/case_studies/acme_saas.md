# Case Study: Acme SaaS

## Company Background

Acme SaaS is a B2B project management platform headquartered in Austin, TX, serving 1,200+ mid-market customers across North America and EMEA. With 180 employees and $28M ARR at the time of engagement, Acme was scaling rapidly but struggling to keep GTM execution aligned with their growth targets.

## Challenge

Acme's revenue team was running outbound, content, and lead nurture programs across five disconnected tools. Their 12-person marketing team spent an average of 14 hours per week on manual list building, personalization, and CRM updates. Key problems included:

- **Slow deal desk cycles**: Average 11-day turnaround from MQL to first outbound touch.
- **Low personalization at scale**: Only 22% of outbound emails included account-specific research.
- **Inconsistent messaging**: Three BDRs, three different value propositions with no central governance.
- **CRM data decay**: 38% of contact records were stale within 90 days.

## Solution

Acme deployed OmniGTM to automate their outbound and lead nurture workflows. The implementation included:

1. **Automated ICP scoring and account selection** using firmographic and technographic data enrichment.
2. **AI-powered research and personalization** generating account-specific email drafts with pain-point mapping.
3. **Centralized messaging governance** with approved value propositions and objection handling loaded into the knowledge base.
4. **Continuous CRM enrichment** keeping contact and account records current without manual effort.

The rollout took 3 weeks from kickoff to full production, with the first automated sequences live in week 2.

## Results

After 90 days of running OmniGTM:

- **52% reduction in deal desk cycle time** — from 11 days to 5.3 days MQL-to-first-touch.
- **3.4x increase in personalized outbound volume** — from 340 to 1,156 personalized emails per month.
- **41% improvement in reply rates** — from 4.2% to 5.9% across all outbound sequences.
- **27% reduction in CRM data decay** — stale records dropped from 38% to 11%.
- **$1.2M in influenced pipeline** attributed to OmniGTM-generated sequences in the first quarter.
- **LLM cost per session**: $0.06 average, well under the $0.08 target.
