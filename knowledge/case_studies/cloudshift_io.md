# Case Study: CloudShift.io

## Company Background

CloudShift.io is a cloud infrastructure company headquartered in Seattle, WA, providing multi-cloud orchestration and cost optimization tools for mid-market and enterprise IT teams. With 350 employees and $51M ARR at the time of engagement, CloudShift serves 1,100+ customers across technology, financial services, and manufacturing sectors. Their product portfolio includes CloudShift Orchestrator (multi-cloud management), CloudShift FinOps (cost optimization), and CloudShift Secure (compliance automation).

## Challenge

CloudShift's enterprise sales motion was stalling due to deal desk bottlenecks. Enterprise customers — representing 62% of new ARR — demanded custom pricing configurations that combined platform fees, per-cloud-account charges, and feature-level entitlements. Key problems included:

- **5-day average quote cycle**: Every enterprise deal required manual involvement from Sales, Solutions Engineering, Finance, and Legal. The average quote took 5 business days from initial request to customer delivery.
- **Deal desk as a single point of failure**: Two deal desk analysts processed all enterprise quotes. When either was out, the queue backed up by 2-3 additional days.
- **Missed deal velocity**: 18% of enterprise opportunities went cold during the quoting process, with prospects citing "too slow" as the reason in post-loss surveys.
- **Sub-optimal packaging**: Sales reps defaulted to standard packages because custom configurations were too time-consuming, leaving 20-30% of potential deal value on the table.
- **Approval bottleneck**: Discount approvals required VP-level sign-off for anything above 15%, adding 1-2 days for 41% of enterprise deals.

## Solution

CloudShift deployed OmniGTM's CPQ and pricing automation platform to transform their enterprise quoting process:

1. **Guided selling and configuration engine** allowing sales reps to build custom multi-product bundles through an interactive workflow, with real-time pricing and margin calculations.
2. **Dynamic discount governance** with automated approval routing based on deal size, discount depth, and strategic account designation — eliminating manual VP approvals for 78% of deals.
3. **Template-based custom pricing** with 12 pre-approved enterprise packaging templates that covered 90% of custom request patterns, with configurable parameters for cloud account count, feature tiers, and contract length.
4. **Real-time margin analysis** showing reps the impact of each configuration decision on deal margin, CLTV projection, and commission — enabling informed negotiation without finance involvement.
5. **One-click proposal generation** producing branded, customer-ready proposals with pricing breakdowns, ROI projections, and contract terms directly from the quoting interface.

Implementation took 5 weeks, with parallel running against the existing manual process for 2 weeks before full cutover.

## Results

After 120 days of running OmniGTM:

- **Quote cycle reduced from 5 days to 4 hours** — a 96% reduction driven by self-service configuration and automated approvals.
- **23% increase in average enterprise deal size** — from $187K to $230K ACV, attributed to reps confidently building optimized multi-product bundles instead of defaulting to standard packages.
- **Enterprise pipeline velocity increased by 34%** — faster quotes meant more deals progressing per quarter, with the pipeline stage from "proposal" to "negotiation" compressing from 12 days to 5 days.
- **Lost deals due to slow quoting dropped from 18% to 3%** — near-elimination of quote-cycle-related deal loss.
- **Deal desk capacity freed**: The 2 deal desk analysts shifted from quote processing to strategic deal advisory, supporting complex multi-year and partnership deals.
- **Discount governance improved**: Average enterprise discount dropped from 22% to 17% without impacting win rates, adding $1.8M in annual margin.
- **Sales rep ramp time reduced by 40%** — new reps could produce accurate enterprise quotes in their second week instead of month two.
