# Case Study: FinTech Co

## Company Background

FinTech Co is a usage-based billing and payments platform serving 800+ SaaS companies, headquartered in San Francisco. At $42M ARR with 260 employees, FinTech Co provides metered billing infrastructure that enables their customers to adopt consumption-based pricing models.

## Growth Challenge

Despite strong product-market fit, FinTech Co's GTM engine was struggling to keep pace with their ambitious growth targets. The usage-based model created unique challenges:

- **Complex buyer education**: Prospects needed to understand consumption pricing before evaluating FinTech Co's platform, lengthening sales cycles to 67 days on average.
- **Multi-persona selling**: Deals required buy-in from Engineering, Finance, and Product teams — each needing different messaging.
- **Competitive noise**: Six new entrants in the billing infrastructure space in 18 months made differentiation harder.
- **Content bottleneck**: The 4-person marketing team could only produce 2 case studies and 3 technical blog posts per quarter, far below what was needed to support the sales cycle.

## Billing Complexity

FinTech Co's own pricing was usage-based, which meant their outbound strategy needed to account for:

- Prospects at different stages of pricing model maturity (flat-rate, tiered, considering usage-based).
- Technical evaluators who cared about API reliability and metering accuracy.
- Finance stakeholders focused on revenue recognition and billing compliance.
- Product leaders interested in pricing experimentation and A/B testing.

A one-size-fits-all outbound approach was generating a 1.8% reply rate — well below industry benchmarks.

## Solution

FinTech Co deployed OmniGTM with a focus on multi-persona, multi-stage outbound:

1. **Persona-aware account research**: The agent analyzed each prospect's current pricing model, tech stack, and recent product launches to determine pricing maturity stage.
2. **Dynamic sequence routing**: Contacts were routed into persona-specific sequences (Engineering, Finance, Product) with messaging tailored to each stakeholder's priorities.
3. **Competitive intelligence automation**: Job posting analysis and pricing page monitoring identified prospects actively evaluating billing solutions.
4. **Knowledge base integration**: Approved technical content, competitive battlecards, and persona-specific value propositions ensured consistent, accurate messaging.

## Results

After 120 days:

- **Sales cycle reduced by 31%** — from 67 days to 46 days average, driven by better-qualified and better-educated prospects.
- **Reply rate increased to 6.7%** — up from 1.8%, a 3.7x improvement.
- **Pipeline generated**: $3.8M in new qualified pipeline from OmniGTM sequences.
- **Content leverage**: Knowledge base entries were referenced in 89% of generated emails, ensuring messaging consistency across 14 BDRs.
- **Multi-persona coverage**: 73% of target accounts had at least 2 personas engaged within the first sequence cycle, up from 28%.
- **Competitive win rate improvement**: 18% increase in competitive deals won, attributed to faster response times and better-informed battlecard usage.
- **Cost efficiency**: Average LLM cost of $0.07 per session across all persona variants.
